# FDA MoCRA Compliance for Cosmetics Brands: Everything You Need (2026)

*Last updated: May 2026 — by Cleo Labs*

For 80 years, US cosmetics were the most lightly regulated category in personal care. The FDA could not require facility registration, could not demand product listings, and could not order recalls. **That ended on December 29, 2022**, when President Biden signed the **Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act (MoCRA)** into law. By the end of 2026, every cosmetic brand selling into the United States must be MoCRA-compliant or risk an FDA enforcement action.

This guide explains exactly what MoCRA requires, who is exempt, what the costs are, and the deadlines you still have time to hit.

## What MoCRA actually does

MoCRA amends the Federal Food, Drug, and Cosmetic Act (FD&C Act) to add cosmetic-specific requirements. The core obligations:

1. **Facility registration** with FDA
2. **Product listing** with FDA
3. **Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP)** standards (enforceable Dec 2026)
4. **Adverse Event Reporting** within 15 business days for serious events
5. **Safety substantiation** for every product
6. **Records access** by FDA on request
7. **Mandatory recall authority** for the FDA (new)
8. **Labeling: fragrance allergen disclosure**, professional product labeling

Plus FDA's first-time **mandatory recall authority** — they can now order recalls instead of merely requesting them.

## Who is in scope

Any **"Responsible Person"** that manufactures or packages a cosmetic product **distributed in interstate commerce** in the US. This includes:

- US-based manufacturers
- US-based brand owners that outsource manufacturing
- **Foreign manufacturers** whose products are imported (they need a **US Agent**)
- Private-label brands (the entity whose name appears on the label is the Responsible Person)

## Small business exemption — the trap

MoCRA exempts "small businesses" — those whose average gross annual sales of cosmetic products for the previous 3-year period are **less than $1 million** — from:

- Facility registration
- Product listing
- GMP

But the exemption **does not apply** if the product is:

- Designed to come into contact with mucous membranes (lip products, eye products)
- Designed for routine use on **broken skin**
- Internally applied
- Designed to **alter appearance for more than 24 hours**
- An **injectable**
- Designed to penetrate skin (transdermal patches)
- Made of a substance that the FDA classifies as Class III

So a $300K/yr lipstick brand is **not exempt**. A $300K/yr body cream brand probably is.

Adverse event reporting and safety substantiation apply to **everyone**.

## The deadlines you need to know

| Obligation | Deadline |
|---|---|
| Facility registration (new) | Within 60 days of starting manufacturing |
| Facility registration (existing) | Was: Dec 29, 2023 → extended to Jul 1, 2024 |
| Product listing (new product) | Within 120 days of marketing |
| Product listing (existing products) | Was: Dec 29, 2023 → extended to Jul 1, 2024 |
| Renewal of registration / listing | Biennially (every 2 years) |
| Fragrance allergen disclosure rule | Final rule published Jun 2024, compliance **Jul 1, 2026** |
| GMP final rule | **Dec 29, 2026** (FDA deadline to issue, then enforced) |
| Adverse event reporting | **Already in force** (15 business days for serious events) |

If you have not yet registered, you are technically out of compliance. **The window to do it without enforcement scrutiny is closing.**

## How to register a facility

Use the **FDA Cosmetics Direct portal** (https://www.fda.gov/cosmetics) or paper Form FDA 5066. You need:

- Facility name, address, FEI / DUNS (if applicable)
- Type of cosmetic activity (manufacture, package, hold)
- Name + email of person responsible for receiving FDA communications
- **US Agent** name + address if foreign facility
- All brand names manufactured at the facility
- Product categories per the **FDA Cosmetic Product Categories list** (categories 01–13 + sub-categories)

**Cost:** $0 to register. The cost is internal time (~2–4 hours per facility).

## How to list a product

Required per cosmetic product (each unique formulation / brand combination). You need:

- Facility registration number (from the above step)
- Cosmetic product category
- Product name (brand, line, variant)
- Listing of ingredients
- Responsible Person
- Whether the product is for professional use

You can **batch upload** in CSV format via Cosmetics Direct.

**Cost:** $0 to list. Internal time ~30 min per SKU.

## GMP — the big one for Dec 2026

The FDA's **proposed GMP rule** (published Apr 2024 NPRM) is largely modeled on **ISO 22716**. The final rule must be issued no later than **Dec 29, 2026**, and will likely apply 1–2 years after publication.

What the proposed rule will require:

- Documented quality system
- Trained personnel
- Suitable buildings, equipment, processes
- Controls on raw materials, manufacturing, packaging, holding, distribution
- Complaint handling
- Adverse event records
- Internal audits

**If you contract-manufacture in a facility already certified to ISO 22716**, you are 90% ready.

## Adverse Event Reporting (already in force)

A **"serious adverse event"** is defined as one that:
- Results in death
- Causes a life-threatening experience
- Requires inpatient hospitalization
- Causes persistent or significant disability or incapacity
- Causes a congenital anomaly or birth defect
- Or requires medical or surgical intervention to prevent any of the above

You must report to the FDA via **Form FDA 3500A** within **15 business days** of becoming aware.

You must also keep records of **all adverse events (not just serious)** for **6 years** (3 years for small businesses).

## Safety substantiation — the silent requirement

MoCRA Section 608 requires the Responsible Person to ensure and maintain records demonstrating "adequate substantiation of safety" for each cosmetic product.

The FDA has not (yet) defined exactly what counts. In practice, this looks like a **Cosmetic Product Safety Report** of similar shape to the EU CPSR. Most US brands building MoCRA files copy the EU CPSR template.

## Fragrance allergen labeling (Jul 2026)

The FDA's final rule on fragrance allergen labeling (June 2024) tracks the EU's 26 → 81 allergen expansion but with a narrower initial list of **fragrance allergens FDA considers material**. You must:

- Identify on the label any "fragrance allergen" present above the threshold
- Use the FDA-prescribed name (CAS or INCI)
- Comply by **July 1, 2026**

## Cost summary for a typical US-only brand

| Item | One-time | Recurring |
|---|---|---|
| Facility registration | $0 | $0 (renew biennially) |
| Product listing (10 SKUs) | ~5 hrs | ~2 hrs/year |
| Safety substantiation files (10 SKUs) | $5K–$15K (with CPSR-style files) | reviews per reformulation |
| GMP readiness (if not ISO 22716) | $8K–$25K | annual audit $3K–$8K |
| Adverse event reporting system | $500–$2K setup | minimal recurring |
| Label updates for fragrance allergens | $1K–$4K | per redesign |
| **TOTAL** | **$14.5K–$46K** | **$5K–$15K/yr** |

## What can go wrong

- **Selling without facility registration → FDA enforcement letter, possible injunction**
- **Late adverse event report → civil penalties, possible criminal referral for repeated violations**
- **No safety substantiation file → FDA can issue mandatory recall, plus state attorneys general can sue under unfair-trade laws**
- **Mislabeled fragrance allergens → recall + class-action exposure**
- **US Agent missing for foreign manufacturer → CBP can refuse entry**

## How MoCRA interacts with state laws

MoCRA does not preempt state law. **California (Prop 65, Toxic-Free Cosmetics Act)**, **New York (1,4-dioxane limits)**, and **Washington (Toxic-Free Cosmetics Act 2023)** all add additional substance restrictions on top of MoCRA. You comply with the strictest applicable rule for each market.
