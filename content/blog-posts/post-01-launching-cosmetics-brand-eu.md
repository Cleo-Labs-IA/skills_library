## Why the EU is its own universe

The EU is the strictest major cosmetics market in the world. Stricter than the US, stricter than the UK (which now diverges slightly), stricter than most of Asia outside Japan and Korea. The reason is simple: there is a single regulation (1223/2009), enforced harmoniously across 27 member states, with a unified notification portal (CPNP), backed by an EU-wide product safety regulator network and a database of restricted/banned substances that updates several times per year.

The flip side: once a product is compliant in the EU, it is compliant in **27 markets and ~450 million consumers**. That is why almost every serious global beauty brand uses EU compliance as their reference template.

## The 7 pillars of EU cosmetics compliance

Every cosmetic product placed on the EU market must satisfy seven obligations. Miss one, get pulled off shelves. Pass all seven, you can sell.

1. **Designate an EU Responsible Person (RP)**
2. **Compile a Product Information File (PIF)**
3. **Have a Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR) signed**
4. **Comply with Annex II to VI substance lists**
5. **Notify the Cosmetic Product Notification Portal (CPNP)**
6. **Apply compliant labeling** (incl. INCI, allergens, warnings, RP address)
7. **Operate under Good Manufacturing Practice (GMP) ISO 22716**

Let's go through each.

### 1. EU Responsible Person — your most important hire

Article 4 of Reg 1223/2009 requires a **single Responsible Person established in the EU** for each cosmetic product. The RP is the legal counterpart of the brand toward EU authorities. They hold the PIF, file the CPNP notification, and answer regulators within 72 hours of any inquiry.

If your company is established in the EU, you can be your own RP. Most US, UK, Australian, and Asian brands appoint an external RP service.

**Typical RP service costs (2026):**
- Solo entry-level RP service: €70–90/month flat
- Agency RP (Obelis, Marcus Evans, Cosmesigner): €99–250/month + per-SKU fees
- White-label RP via a 3PL: bundled at €0 but limited to that 3PL's network

**The mandate letter:** a written document signed by both parties stating the RP's scope of authority. Keep it in the PIF.

### 2. The Product Information File (PIF)

Article 11 requires that for **each cosmetic product placed on the market**, the RP holds a PIF, accessible to authorities for **10 years after the last batch was placed on the market**.

Minimum PIF contents:
- Description of the cosmetic product (brand name, presentation, intended use)
- The CPSR (Annex I to Reg 1223/2009)
- Description of manufacturing method + declaration of GMP compliance
- Proof of any claimed effect (efficacy data) where the claim's nature justifies it
- Data on animal testing carried out by the manufacturer (post-2013 ban)

The PIF is **digital + paper**. Most brands keep it in a versioned shared drive (Notion, Box, Google Drive with versioning) with audit trail. The PIF travels with each formula. If you reformulate, you start a new PIF revision.

### 3. The Cosmetic Product Safety Report (CPSR)

The CPSR is the heart of the PIF. It is the **safety assessment of the finished product**, structured in **Part A (safety information) and Part B (safety assessment)** per Annex I.

Part B must be **signed by a qualified safety assessor** — typically a person with a pharmacy, toxicology, or medicine degree, certified per Article 10 of the regulation.

**CPSR cost (2026):** typically **€400–€1,200 per formula** in the EU. Faster turnarounds (5 business days) cost more. Plan **2–4 weeks** for the typical assessment.

The assessor will ask for:
- Full quantitative + qualitative formula
- All raw material specifications + safety data sheets (MSDS)
- Microbiological challenge test (Preservation Efficacy Test, ISO 11930)
- Stability + compatibility data
- Toxicological profile of each ingredient at use concentration
- Impurities and pesticide residues data
- Packaging compatibility

### 4. The substance lists (Annexes II to VI)

The regulation contains four critical annexes that change several times per year:

- **Annex II** — banned substances (~1,700 entries). If your formula contains one, it cannot be sold. Period.
- **Annex III** — restricted substances (~290 entries). Allowed with concentration / use-condition restrictions. Example: salicylic acid limited to 2.0% in rinse-off, 0.5% leave-on.
- **Annex IV** — permitted colorants
- **Annex V** — permitted preservatives
- **Annex VI** — permitted UV filters

Founders' favorite traps:
- **Retinol restricted Nov 2025**: max 0.3% body / 0.05% face per Reg 2024/996
- **Lilial (Butylphenyl Methylpropional) banned** since Mar 2022
- **Kojic acid restricted Feb 2025**: max 1% in face/hand products
- **Methyl-2-Octynoate restricted**: trace allergens
- **Cyclomethicone (D4/D5/D6)**: restricted under REACH for leave-on

This is where `substance-screening` and `product-compliance` skills earn their keep — they cross-check every ingredient against the latest Annex versions in seconds.

### 5. CPNP notification

Before placing the product on the market, the RP must notify the **Cosmetic Product Notification Portal**, a free EU-run system. The notification includes:

- Product category
- Product name (per member state if different)
- RP name + address
- Importer details
- Country of origin (for imports)
- Member state where placed on market
- Frame formulation
- Presence of CMR substances or nanomaterials
- Photo of the original packaging + label

CPNP login: **EU Login** account, then SAAS access. There is a **specific CPNP user manual** per Commission Implementing Decision. Each notification gets a unique **CPNP number** — keep it in your records and on retailer onboarding sheets.

CPNP is free. Time to file: **30–60 minutes** per SKU once the data is gathered.

### 6. Labeling (Article 19)

Mandatory information on **both immediate packaging and outer packaging** (if any):

- Name and address of the EU **Responsible Person** (street address, not PO box)
- Country of origin (for imports)
- Nominal content in **metric units (g, mL)**
- Date of minimum durability (BBD) if <30 months; or PAO symbol if ≥30 months
- Particular precautions (warnings)
- **Batch number / lot code**
- Function (unless obvious)
- **Ingredient list in INCI nomenclature**, in descending order of weight at time of incorporation, ingredients <1% in any order at the end. Colorants in any order, prefixed by "+/-" if "may contain".
- **26 allergens** (set in Annex III) declared separately if above **0.001% in rinse-off** or **0.01% in leave-on**
- **Language**: at least one official language of each member state where placed on market

**The 26 allergens went to 81** under Reg 2023/1545, with a transition until **31 July 2028** for re-labeling. Plan ahead.

### 7. GMP — ISO 22716

Article 8 requires manufacture per **good manufacturing practice**, with **ISO 22716** as the harmonized standard. If you contract-manufacture, the contract manufacturer ("CMO" or "Façonnier" in French) must hold ISO 22716 certification or equivalent. Ask for the audit certificate.

If you self-manufacture, you need:
- Premises designed for cosmetics (separation, hygiene)
- Trained personnel
- Documented procedures (cleaning, sanitation, deviations)
- Quality control on raw materials, in-process, finished
- Complaint handling + recall procedure

Most small brands skip self-manufacture and use a **CMO**.

## What can go wrong (and what it costs)

- **Wrong RP address on label**: relabeling all stock — €500–€5K + lost retail weeks
- **Missing CPNP for one SKU**: Amazon EU delist within 30 days
- **Banned ingredient in formula**: pull all stock + reformulation cost ~€8K + 8 weeks
- **Allergen mis-declared**: relabel + member-state warning + brand-trust damage
- **No PIF held by RP**: civil fine up to €30K per offense + product seizure
- **Claim without proof**: Commission can require retraction + fines
