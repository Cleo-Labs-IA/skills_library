# Product Frameworks Catalog

## EU Cosmetics Regulation 1223/2009

- **Scope**: Any substance or mixture intended for contact with external body parts (skin, hair, nails, lips, external genitalia) or teeth/mucous membranes for cleaning, perfuming, changing appearance, protecting, or keeping in good condition. Includes soap, shampoo, toothpaste, deodorant.
- **Structure**: Safety assessment (CPSR by qualified person), Product Information File (PIF), CPNP notification, Responsible Person (EU-based), labeling (Art. 19), substance annexes (II=banned ~1,698; III=restricted ~300; IV=colorants; V=preservatives; VI=UV filters).
- **Key obligations**: No animal testing (including for ingredients). Serious undesirable effects (SUE) reporting. Good Manufacturing Practice (ISO 22716). Nanomaterial notification (6 months before placing on market).
- **Penalties**: Member state defined. France: up to EUR 150,000 + 2 years imprisonment. Product withdrawal/recall powers.
- **Pitfalls**: CPSR not done or not by qualified assessor; labeling missing mandatory elements; CPNP notification incomplete; claims not substantiated per EU Claims Regulation; fragrance allergens not individually listed.
- **Timeline**: First product 8-16 weeks (safety assessment is the bottleneck). Subsequent products 2-4 weeks if similar formulation.
- **Cost**: EUR 3,000-10,000 per product (CPSR + testing + CPNP + labeling).

## REACH (Regulation (EC) No 1907/2006)

- **Scope**: ALL chemical substances manufactured in or imported to EU above 1 tonne/year. Applies to substances, mixtures, AND articles (if substances are intended to be released or are on SVHC Candidate List at >0.1% w/w).
- **Structure**: Registration (manufacturer/importer responsibility), Evaluation (ECHA), Authorization (for SVHCs), Restriction (Annex XVII).
- **Key for product companies**: SVHC notification if >0.1% w/w in article AND >1 tonne/year. Annex XVII restrictions (e.g., nickel in jewelry, azo dyes in textiles, lead in consumer products). SCIP database notification for articles containing SVHCs.
- **Penalties**: Member state defined. Germany: up to EUR 50,000. France: up to EUR 75,000.
- **Pitfalls**: Assuming REACH only applies to chemical manufacturers (it applies to importers and article producers too); not checking SVHC Candidate List updates (updated twice/year); ignoring SCIP notification duty.
- **Timeline**: SVHC check 1-2 weeks. Registration (if needed) 3-18 months.
- **Cost**: SVHC screening EUR 500-2,000. Full registration EUR 10,000-100,000+ (rare for product companies -- usually supplier's responsibility).

## CLP (Regulation (EC) No 1272/2008)

- **Scope**: Classification, Labeling, Packaging of chemical substances and mixtures sold in EU. Applies to raw materials and finished products that are mixtures (e.g., cleaning products, paints).
- **Structure**: GHS-aligned hazard classification. Mandatory hazard pictograms, signal words, H-statements, P-statements on label. Safety Data Sheet (SDS) for professional/industrial use.
- **Key for product companies**: Cleaning products, paints, adhesives, some cosmetic intermediates. Consumer products must have child-resistant closures if certain hazard classes apply.
- **Penalties**: Member state fines + product withdrawal.
- **Pitfalls**: Classifying mixtures incorrectly; missing hazard pictograms; not updating when classification criteria change (ATP adaptations).
- **Timeline**: Classification 2-4 weeks. Labeling update 1-2 weeks.

## FDA MoCRA (Modernization of Cosmetics Regulation Act, 2022)

- **Scope**: Cosmetics sold in the US. Effective from various dates in 2024-2025.
- **Structure**: Facility registration (biennial), product listing, adverse event reporting (15 days for serious), GMP requirements (phased in), safety substantiation, fragrance allergen disclosure (future rulemaking).
- **Key changes from prior US law**: Previously minimal FDA oversight. MoCRA adds mandatory registration, listing, adverse event reporting, GMP, records access, and recall authority.
- **Penalties**: FDA can issue warning letters, injunctions, seizures, and mandatory recalls.
- **Pitfalls**: Assuming US cosmetics are unregulated (no longer true post-MoCRA); not registering facility; not having adverse event reporting system; confusing cosmetic vs OTC drug classification.
- **Timeline**: Registration 1 day (once documentation ready). GMP implementation varies.
- **Cost**: Registration is free. GMP implementation EUR 2,000-10,000 for small manufacturer.

## California Proposition 65

- **Scope**: Any product sold in California containing a substance known to the State to cause cancer or reproductive harm. ~900+ substances on the list.
- **Structure**: Warning required if exposure above "safe harbor" levels (No Significant Risk Level for carcinogens, Maximum Allowable Dose Level for reproductive toxins). Safe harbor levels not available for all substances.
- **Key for product companies**: Applies to cosmetics, food, supplements, electronics, toys -- anything sold in CA. E-commerce sellers with CA customers are covered.
- **Penalties**: Fines up to $2,500/day per violation. Private enforcement lawsuits (bounty hunters) are the real threat -- average settlement $30,000-$100,000+.
- **Pitfalls**: Thinking it only applies to California companies (it applies to anyone selling IN California); not having safe harbor data for your substances; ignoring the private enforcement mechanism.
- **Timeline**: Assessment 2-4 weeks. Warning label implementation 1 week.
- **Cost**: Assessment EUR 500-2,000. Settlements if non-compliant: USD 30,000-100,000+.

## EU General Product Safety Regulation (GPSR) 2023/988

- **Scope**: ALL consumer products placed on the EU market. Replaces the old General Product Safety Directive (GPSD). Effective December 13, 2024.
- **Structure**: General safety requirement. Risk assessment obligation. Technical documentation. Traceability requirements. Incident reporting through Safety Gate. Online marketplace obligations.
- **Key changes**: Applies to ALL products (not just those without sector-specific regulation). Requires product safety contact point on product/packaging. Strengthens online marketplace responsibilities. Internal risk analysis mandatory.
- **Penalties**: Member state defined, aligned with market surveillance regulation (up to 4% of turnover in some jurisdictions).
- **Pitfalls**: Assuming sector-specific regulation (like 1223/2009 for cosmetics) exempts you from GPSR entirely (GPSR fills gaps); not having a product safety contact; not having an internal risk analysis process.
- **Timeline**: Must be compliant since Dec 2024. Setup 4-8 weeks.

## CE Marking

- **Scope**: Products covered by EU directives/regulations that require CE marking. NOT a quality mark -- it is a declaration that the product meets EU safety/health/environmental requirements.
- **Key directives**: LVD 2014/35/EU (electrical safety), EMC 2014/30/EU (electromagnetic compatibility), RED 2014/53/EU (radio equipment), RoHS 2011/65/EU (hazardous substances), Toy Safety 2009/48/EC, Machinery 2006/42/EC, Medical Devices MDR 2017/745.
- **Process**: Identify applicable directives -> apply harmonized standards -> test/assess -> prepare technical documentation -> write Declaration of Conformity -> affix CE mark.
- **Notified Body**: Required for some product categories (toys, certain electronics, medical devices Class IIa+). Self-declaration allowed for many consumer electronics.
- **Penalties**: Selling non-CE-marked products where CE is required = illegal. Fines, product seizure, criminal prosecution in some member states.
- **Pitfalls**: Affixing CE without actual conformity assessment; using wrong harmonized standards; not updating when directives change; missing importer obligations (must be on label); CE mark wrong size or proportions.
- **Timeline**: Self-declaration 6-12 weeks. Notified Body 12-24 weeks.
- **Cost**: Self-declaration EUR 3,000-10,000 (testing). Notified Body EUR 8,000-25,000+.

## UKCA Marking

- **Scope**: Products placed on the GB market (not Northern Ireland, which follows EU rules under the Windsor Framework).
- **Structure**: Mirrors CE marking but under UK Designated Standards (largely identical to EU harmonized standards for now). UK-recognized conformity assessment bodies.
- **Current status**: UK has indefinitely extended acceptance of CE marking for most product categories. UKCA marking is technically required but CE marking remains valid. This may change.
- **Pitfalls**: Not appointing a UK Responsible Person (required for imports); assuming CE = automatic UKCA acceptance forever (policy could change); Northern Ireland follows EU rules, not GB.
- **Timeline**: Similar to CE if starting from scratch.

## RoHS (Directive 2011/65/EU)

- **Scope**: Electrical and electronic equipment (EEE) placed on EU market.
- **Structure**: 10 restricted substances with maximum concentration values: Lead (0.1%), Mercury (0.01%), Cadmium (0.01%), Hexavalent chromium (0.1%), PBB (0.1%), PBDE (0.1%), DEHP (0.1%), BBP (0.1%), DBP (0.1%), DIBP (0.1%).
- **Key for product companies**: Applies to finished electronics AND components. Testing required for compliance. Part of CE marking (requires Declaration of Conformity including RoHS).
- **Pitfalls**: Forgetting the 4 phthalates added in 2019 (DEHP, BBP, DBP, DIBP); not testing sub-components; assuming supplier compliance without verification.
- **Timeline**: Testing 2-4 weeks. Usually done as part of CE marking process.

## WEEE (Directive 2012/19/EU)

- **Scope**: Producers of electrical and electronic equipment in EU.
- **Structure**: Producer registration in each EU member state where products are sold. Financing of collection and recycling. Crossed-out wheelie bin symbol on products. Annual reporting.
- **Key for product companies**: If you sell electronics in the EU, you must register as a producer with each member state authority or use a producer compliance scheme (like take-e-way, ERP, Landbell).
- **Penalties**: Member state defined. Significant fines for non-registration.
- **Pitfalls**: Not registering in every member state; not including WEEE cost in pricing; not putting the crossed-out wheelie bin on the product.
- **Timeline**: Registration 2-4 weeks per country. Compliance scheme setup 1-2 weeks.
- **Cost**: Registration fees vary. Compliance scheme EUR 200-2,000/year per country (depends on volume).

## EPR / REP (Extended Producer Responsibility for Packaging)

- **Scope**: Any company that places packaged products on the market in EU member states. Country-specific implementation (France: CITEO/Loi AGEC, Germany: VerpackG, Spain: ECOEMBES, etc.).
- **Structure**: Register as a producer in each country. Declare packaging volumes (by material type and weight). Pay eco-contribution. In some countries, include specific recycling symbols (e.g., Triman in France).
- **Key for product companies**: If you sell a physical product in the EU, your packaging is subject to EPR. Even e-commerce sellers shipping from outside the EU.
- **Penalties**: Germany: up to EUR 200,000 for non-registration. France: fines + ban on sales.
- **Pitfalls**: Not registering in every country where you sell; not declaring correct packaging weights; forgetting shipping packaging (it counts too); not including correct recycling symbols.
- **Timeline**: Registration 1-4 weeks per country.
- **Cost**: Registration EUR 50-500/year per country. Eco-contribution EUR 0.01-0.20/unit (depends on packaging material and weight).

## EN 71 (Toy Safety)

- **Scope**: Toys intended for children under 14. Covered by EU Toy Safety Directive 2009/48/EC.
- **Structure**: EN 71-1 (mechanical/physical), EN 71-2 (flammability), EN 71-3 (migration of certain elements), EN 71-4 to 71-14 (chemistry, activity toys, etc.). Plus EN 62115 for electric toys.
- **Testing**: Must be performed by a Notified Body for EU Type Examination. Third-party testing mandatory.
- **Pitfalls**: Assuming "not a toy" avoids the directive (if a child would reasonably play with it, it may be classified as a toy); not testing all applicable EN 71 parts; missing chemical migration limits (EN 71-3 has 19 elements with specific limits).
- **Timeline**: Testing 8-16 weeks. Certification 12-20 weeks total.
- **Cost**: EUR 2,000-8,000 for standard toy. More for electronic toys.

## CPSIA (Consumer Product Safety Improvement Act, US)

- **Scope**: Children's products (designed for children 12 and under) in the US.
- **Structure**: Lead content limit (100 ppm total, 90 ppm in paint). Phthalate limits (8 restricted phthalates, 0.1%). Mandatory third-party testing by CPSC-accepted lab. Children's Product Certificate (CPC). Permanent tracking label.
- **Key for product companies**: ANY product marketed to children in the US. Includes toys, clothing, furniture, accessories.
- **Penalties**: Up to $100,000 per violation, $15,000,000 max. Criminal penalties possible.
- **Pitfalls**: Thinking CPSIA only applies to toys (it covers ALL children's products); not using CPSC-accepted laboratory; missing the tracking label requirement; not issuing a CPC.
- **Timeline**: Testing 4-8 weeks.
- **Cost**: USD 1,000-5,000 per product for testing + CPC.

## EU Battery Regulation (2023/1542)

- **Scope**: All batteries placed on EU market. Replaces Battery Directive. Phased implementation 2024-2027.
- **Structure**: Carbon footprint declaration (2025+), recycled content requirements (2028+), performance/durability requirements, labeling (QR code linking to battery passport), collection/recycling targets, due diligence for raw materials.
- **Key for product companies**: If your product contains a battery, this regulation applies. Portable batteries, industrial batteries, EV batteries, LMT batteries.
- **Pitfalls**: Ignoring phased implementation dates; not planning for QR code/battery passport requirement; not registering as producer for battery EPR.
- **Timeline**: Already in effect (phased). Full compliance required by 2027.

## FCC Part 15 (US)

- **Scope**: Radio frequency devices in the US. Two categories: intentional radiators (WiFi, Bluetooth) and unintentional radiators (all digital devices).
- **Structure**: Intentional radiators need FCC Equipment Authorization (certification by TCB). Unintentional radiators need FCC compliance statement (self-declaration with testing).
- **Pitfalls**: Not testing for EMC compliance; missing FCC ID label requirement for intentional radiators; not including required FCC statements in user manual.
- **Timeline**: Testing 4-8 weeks. TCB certification 2-4 weeks additional.
- **Cost**: USD 3,000-10,000 for testing. USD 1,000-3,000 for TCB certification.

## Appendix: Data/IT Frameworks (if you also handle customer data)

### ISO 27001:2022
- ISMS for organizations handling data. 93 controls, 4 themes. 3-year cert with annual surveillance. Relevant if selling e-commerce with customer accounts.

### SOC 2 Type II
- Service organizations processing customer data. US-origin. Relevant if you provide a SaaS component alongside physical products.

### GDPR
- Personal data of EU individuals. Relevant for e-commerce customer data, marketing lists, analytics.

### PCI-DSS v4.0.1
- Cardholder data. Relevant if processing credit cards directly (most small companies use Stripe/payment processors that handle this).
