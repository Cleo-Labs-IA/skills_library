## Value Propositions

We sell three different products to three different buyers. Same toolkit, different framing.

### For D2C founders (operators)
> **Stop choosing between "ship now and pray" or "spend €50k on consultants."** `skills_library` gives your AI assistant instant expertise on every market you want to sell into. Get a compliance verdict in minutes, not weeks.

*The hook:* compliance for the price of a `git clone`.

### For developers (technical buyers)
> **Compliance as a skill, not a service.** Drop `skills_library` into Claude Code and ship features that already know the regulations. MIT-licensed, no vendor lock-in, no API keys required for the open-source tier.

*The hook:* finally, regulatory knowledge that lives in `~/.claude/skills/` instead of a Notion doc.

### For regulatory pros (consultants & in-house teams)
> **Stop drafting the same memo for the 50th client.** `skills_library` automates the 80% of compliance work that's just lookup — leaving you free to do the 20% that actually requires judgment. Cleo Legal API gives you the live regulatory feed underneath.

*The hook:* multiply your seat count without multiplying your hours.

## Key Messages

If a piece of media communicates anything about us, it should communicate these three things:

### 1. It's open source. For real.
MIT license, full GitHub repo, no premium gates on the skills themselves. The paid product is the *infrastructure* (Cleo Legal API), not the skills. This matters because it's how the dev-tool default gets set.

### 2. It's production-grade compliance, not vibes.
Every skill is built on real substance databases — CosIng, ECHA, REACH, FDA, MHLW, KCS, Health Canada. It's not "AI guessing the rules." It's "AI applying the rules."

### 3. It's the first of its kind.
There has never been an open-source compliance skills library for AI agents. GRC tools are dashboards. Consultancies are people. We are infrastructure.

## What we say vs. what we don't say

### ✅ We say
- "Compliance guidance"
- "Regulatory intelligence"
- "Skills that know the rules"
- "Helps you know what to check"
- "Co-pilot for compliance work"
- "Reduces the cost of compliance research"
- "Open-source toolkit"

### ❌ We never say
- ~~"Legal advice"~~ — we are not attorneys
- ~~"Guaranteed compliance"~~ — no software guarantees compliance, full stop
- ~~"Replaces your consultant"~~ — it doesn't, and saying so creates risk for users
- ~~"Certified"~~ — we are not a certification body
- ~~"Approved by [authority]"~~ — we ingest authority data, we are not endorsed
- ~~"You don't need a lawyer"~~ — sometimes you absolutely do

### Why this matters
Compliance is a regulated space. The wrong claim creates liability for our users *and* for us. We are aggressive about marketing the product and conservative about claims of legal effect. **Every media mention should reflect that line.**

## Positioning — what we're not

`skills_library` exists in a crowded "compliance" market. Here's where we sit.

### We are not a GRC tool
Vanta, Drata, Secureframe — they help SOC2/ISO/HIPAA *security* compliance for SaaS companies. We do *product* compliance for physical goods (cosmetics, food, electronics, etc.). Completely different category.

### We are not a regulatory consultancy
Compliance & Risks, ChemicalWatch, Sphera — they sell expertise and dashboards to enterprise. We sell open-source skills to anyone with a terminal. Different price, different buyer, different motion.

### We are not a generic AI assistant
ChatGPT, Claude.ai, Gemini — they can *talk about* compliance. They don't have structured knowledge of CosIng or the FDA database. We give AI agents the structured knowledge layer they're missing.

### We are
**The open-source compliance skills layer.** Like Hugging Face for ML models, like npm for JavaScript libraries — but for regulatory expertise.

If you have to compare us to one company in your headline, **Hugging Face for compliance skills** is the framing that travels best.
