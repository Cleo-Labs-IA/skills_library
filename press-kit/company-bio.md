## Long — 300 words

**Cleo Labs** is a Paris-based regulatory intelligence company building the open-source compliance layer for the AI agent era. Founded in 2024 by Naomie Halioua, the company exists to compress decades of fragmented regulatory expertise into infrastructure that any developer, founder, or AI agent can use in seconds.

Cleo Labs operates two complementary products. The **Cleo Legal API** is a regulatory intelligence platform tracking 1,479 legal sources across 131 countries, with deep coverage of consumer-facing verticals — cosmetics, food, electronics, toys, textiles, and sustainability. The Pro tier is available at €349/mo and serves compliance teams at consumer brands, legal-tech builders, regulatory affairs consultancies, and AI brand-safety automations. A free Authority tier covers public-sector regulators and academic research.

In **May 2026**, Cleo Labs open-sourced **`skills_library`** — a library of **40 MIT-licensed compliance skills for AI coding agents** including Claude Code. Each skill packages production-grade regulatory knowledge (substance databases like CosIng, ECHA, REACH, FDA; jurisdictional labelling rules; banned/restricted ingredient lists; market-entry requirements) into a format any AI agent can immediately use. The library covers the **EU, US, UK, Japan, Korea, China, and Canada** out of the box and integrates with the Cleo Legal API as a paid upgrade for live regulatory data.

The company's business model is **"free skills, paid infrastructure"** — the open-core model that built Stripe, Vercel, and Linear, applied for the first time to regulatory compliance. Cleo Labs believes every consumer brand will run on an AI compliance co-pilot within five years, and that the open-source default should be a public good rather than a moat held by incumbents.

Cleo Labs is bootstrapped, profitable, and headquartered in Paris. The team is hiring regulatory writers, dev-tool engineers, and partnerships leads.
