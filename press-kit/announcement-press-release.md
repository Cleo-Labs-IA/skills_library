# Cleo Labs Open-Sources `skills_library`: 40 Compliance Skills for AI Coding Agents, the First Library of Its Kind

*MIT-licensed toolkit lets any AI agent answer product-compliance questions across cosmetics, food, electronics, toys, textiles, and sustainability — covering the EU, US, UK, Japan, Korea, China, and Canada out of the box.*

**PARIS, May 28, 2026** — Cleo Labs, the Paris-based regulatory intelligence company, today released **`skills_library`** — the world's first open-source compliance skills library for AI coding agents. The library ships **40 production-ready skills** under the MIT license, covering six consumer-goods verticals and seven major markets, and is available immediately at [github.com/Cleo-Labs-IA/skills_library](https://github.com/Cleo-Labs-IA/skills_library).

`skills_library` is designed to drop into Claude Code and other AI coding agents in under 30 seconds via `git clone`. Once installed, the agent gains expert-level knowledge of substance databases including **CosIng, ECHA, REACH, FDA, MHLW, KCS, and Health Canada**, and can answer real product-compliance questions — *"Can I sell this lipstick in California?"*, *"What's the SPF labelling rule for Korea?"*, *"Is this toy compliant with EU EN 71?"* — with citations and a structured verdict.

The launch addresses a pain point that has remained stubbornly expensive for the entire D2C economy. **"Most D2C founders ship illegally because compliance costs more than their first 1,000 orders combined,"** says Naomie Halioua, founder of Cleo Labs. **"The dirty secret is that 80% of compliance work is just lookup. Everyone's been paying senior-consultant rates for the lookup part. We automated it and gave it away."**

`skills_library` is the open-source layer of Cleo Labs' broader regulatory intelligence stack. The company's commercial product, the **Cleo Legal API** (€349/mo Pro tier), tracks **1,479 legal sources across 131 countries** and powers the live-data integration for paying customers. The open-source skills work offline with bundled regulatory snapshots; users who want real-time updates and unlimited queries connect via a single MCP command (`claude mcp add cleo-legal-api`).

The "free skills, paid infrastructure" model deliberately mirrors the open-core playbook that built Stripe, Vercel, and Linear. **"If your moat depends on hoarding regulatory information that's already public, you don't have a moat — you have a markup,"** Halioua adds. **"Every consumer brand will have an AI compliance co-pilot within five years. We're building the open-source default."**

The launch comes as AI coding agents move into mainstream developer workflows. Skills — a primitive popularized by Anthropic's Claude Code — let AI agents acquire vertical expertise without retraining. `skills_library` is the largest single skill bundle ever released and the first targeting regulatory compliance as a category.

Cleo Labs was founded in 2024 by Naomie Halioua, a former consumer-brand operator who repeatedly watched small teams spend €50,000-to-€500,000 on compliance work before their first sale. The company is bootstrapped, profitable, and headquartered in Paris.
