## Long — 300 words

**Naomie Halioua** is the founder and CEO of **Cleo Labs**, the Paris-based regulatory intelligence company behind `skills_library`, the world's first open-source compliance skills library for AI coding agents.

Naomie's path to Cleo Labs started on the operator side. She spent years working with consumer brands — cosmetics, food, accessories — that all hit the same wall: regulatory compliance is a $50k-to-$500k problem before you've sold your first unit. Big brands have entire affairs teams. Small founders have a Notion doc, a PDF from their consultant, and a prayer.

She founded **Cleo Labs in 2024** to fix that gap by building what the consultants already had — a structured, queryable database of every legal source that matters — and exposing it through interfaces that don't require a JD to use. The result is the **Cleo Legal API** (€349/mo Pro), a regulatory intelligence platform tracking 1,479 legal sources across 131 countries, and now `skills_library` — the open-source library that brings that intelligence directly into AI coding agents like Claude Code.

`skills_library` ships **40 MIT-licensed skills** covering cosmetics, food, electronics, toys, textiles, and sustainability across the EU, US, UK, Japan, Korea, China, and Canada. Each skill packages real regulatory knowledge — substance databases like CosIng, ECHA, REACH, FDA — into a format that any AI agent can use to answer questions like "can I sell this lipstick in California?" with the same rigor as a senior compliance consultant.

Naomie's thesis on open source is simple: "Free skills, paid infrastructure. That's how Stripe, Vercel, Linear all did it." She believes every consumer brand will run on an AI compliance co-pilot within five years — and that the open-source default should be a public good, not a moat.

Naomie is based in Paris. She is available for press, podcasts, and speaking.
